How to Use This Calculator
Important Note

All numbers in this calculator are approximations based on market research and publicly available data. This tool should be used as a reference point only, not as a definitive guide for compensation decisions.

Quick Start Guide
  1. Select the Role you are interested in.
  2. Choose the Seniority Level (e.g., L2-L6).
  3. Select your Country of interest.
  4. Review the dynamically updated Compensation Summary, including annual and monthly salary ranges in both local currency and USD.
  5. Explore the Charts for visual comparisons of salaries, cost of living, taxes, and purchasing power.
  6. View detailed Role Details including responsibilities and skills for the selected level.
  7. Check Location Information for exchange rates, cost of living index, and typical expenses.
Customization & Configuration

  • L2 (Junior): Typically 0-2 years of relevant experience.
  • L3 (Mid-Level): Usually 2-5 years of relevant experience.
  • L4 (Senior): Generally 5-8 years of relevant experience, demonstrating strong individual contribution.
  • L5 (Staff): Around 8-12 years of experience, with technical leadership and/or broader impact.
  • L6 (Principal): 12+ years of experience, often setting technical direction and mentoring at a high level.
Note: Experience ranges are indicative and can vary.

In the "Basic" tab (under "Controls"), you can customize tax percentages for a more accurate estimate of take-home pay:

  • Income Tax (%): Your estimated income tax rate.
  • Social Security (%): Contributions to social security.
  • Other Taxes (%): Any other relevant tax deductions.

Default rates are provided per country and can be reset using the "Reset to Defaults" button.

Switch to the "Overhead" tab to configure and understand employer-side costs. This section helps estimate the total cost for a company to employ someone in the selected location.

  • Monthly Fixed Overhead ($): Base costs like workspace, equipment, software licenses per employee.
  • Employer Tax (%): Company contributions based on salary (e.g., payroll taxes).
  • Workers Comp (%): Workers compensation insurance rates.
  • Other Fees (%): Any other location-specific fees or contributions by the employer.

Default rates are provided and can be reset. The "Company Overhead" card in the results area will display the breakdown based on these settings and the selected salary.

Understanding the Charts
  • Salary Comparison: Compares the selected role/level salary range (USD) with other countries.
  • Cost of Living: Shows a breakdown of key monthly living costs (e.g., rent, utilities) in USD, comparing the selected country with others.
  • Tax Breakdown: Visualizes the estimated deductions (Income Tax, Social Security, Other) from the gross salary to arrive at the net take-home pay.
  • Purchasing Power: Compares the equivalent purchasing power of the selected salary across different countries, adjusted for cost of living.

Remember to use the "Release Notes" (top right) to stay updated on new features and data improvements!
